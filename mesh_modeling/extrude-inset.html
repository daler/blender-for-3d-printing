These are two techniques that can help with adding mesh geometry.

Action Description
E Extrude
I Inset

Exercise

  • Patch the large hole in the torus: select a single vertex and extrude it into the middle of the hole (E)
  • Position the vertex – you may need to move around a bit to get a good view, and possibly use wireframe mode (Z).
  • Fill the hole with faces (F)

In this case, to get a perfect positioning, we’d have to do the math to figure out the position based on the radius of the circle

Exercise

The goal here is to make a rough USB thumb drive shape . . .

  • Back in Object mode, delete the torus.
  • Add a cube
  • Flatten along Z into the rough thickness of a thumb drive
  • Switch to Edit Mode
  • Select the front face of the cube (now more of a rectangular prism...)
  • Extrude the face out to make the “handle” part (E, move mouse, LMB to confirm)
  • With the extruded face selected, inset it a bit (I)
  • With the inset face selected, extrude it to make the outside of the USB connector
  • With the new extruded face selected, inset a bit.
  • Extrude the new inset face backwards to make the socket part of the connector.
